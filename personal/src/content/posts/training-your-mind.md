I know that the mind is the master of human life. The mind controls the body, not
the other way around. So much depends on the ability to master your mind. What does having control
of your mind actually mean?

1. You are able to maintain peace, calm, and lucidity in the midst of turmoil, pain, and fear
2. You are able to live in the present moment without letting the past and future occupy your attention
3. You are able to get yourself in the appropriate state of mind for the situation at hand
4. You are able to observe your impulses, and overcome them. Most importantly fear. You realize that even when you are afraid, you _do_ have a choice still.
5. You trust yourself, because you know you can keep your word and act in accordance with your own values
6. You can face death without fear

How can I go about actually gaining such control? I know I need to find a reliable way to train this capability, like a workout routine. If my muscles can learn to lift more weight, surely my mind can learn to be better. The universe does not want us to suffer, but we must earn our freedom.

I must update this list frequently. I must practice it rigorously. There is nothing more important for my success, happiness, and spiritual fulfillment. _Learn to not fear death, and the rest will come._

## Strategies for Training the Mind

### Consistent Meditation Practice

-   Gives me a powerful tool to call upon when I need calm. Will help me maintain my composure in the midst of suffering, fear, or turmoil. When someone says something that catches me off guard, I've lost my composure many times. I get flustered, I turn red, and the animalistic part of my brain takes over and works on autopilot. Meditation helps me avoid this, and keeps _me_ in charge in challening situations.
-   Increases my overall presence, peace, and calm
-   Makes it easier to be mindful throughout the day

### Mindfulness

-   Practice being aware throughout the day. In every situation. WAKE UP! Are you awake? Are you dreaming at this very moment? Do you even know? Wake up. Introduce a higher dimension of thinking. Yes, you are having a conversation. But do you _know_ you are having a conversation? Don't dream your way through life. When you are awake, you have a choice about how to act.
-   Make it a habit: Ask yourself:
    1. What am I doing now?
    2. How do I feel about what's happening now?
    3. How am I acting? Am I acting how I want to act?
    4. Why am I doing this?
    5. Why does this matter?
-   Is your back straight? Are you speaking slowly? What are your legs, hands, feet, fingers, and tongue doing? What is my mind doing? What is going on inside my body now? Who am I? Is this who I want to be? Am biting my nails? Am I talking to myself? Do I want to be talking to myself? Why am I doing this now? Do I want to be here? If not, why am I here? Am I ready to die at this moment? If not, why not. Wake up. You've been asleep too long. Time is short.
-   The goal is to know what you are doing and why, in order to avoid being dragged along unconciously by your mind, habits, and the universe. Never forget to ask these questions. Make it a habit.

### Hard Physical Exercise

-   Make yourself suffer, and prove that you can overcome it. Lift weights, run sprints, do a sport, whatever may be appealing to you at this point in your life. Just make sure it it hard and it makes you suffer.
-   Learn to watch the pain and still do the action. You're doing pushups? It hurts? That's okay. You know there is something greater within you than this voice that tells you to quit. You've felt it before. You know this.
-   When you're doing something else, ask "is this really worse than the physical suffering I put myself through each week"
-   Learn to conquer the pain. You still have a choice even when you are in pain. Train yourself to never forget this. Pain is a feeling, not a commandment to stop. Respect your body for sure, but conquer the pain. When pain can no longer stop you, you are well on your way to conquering the fear of death, the fear of God, the fear of eternal suffering.

### Doing What I Fear

-   Your mind will tell you not to do it. Remember again: _You have a choice!_ This simple realization gives you all you need to overcome. Yes, you feel fear. It's okay. Do it anyways.
-   When you feel afraid of something, do it. Fear is a sufficient warrant to take action. In doing so, you are sculpting your mind and your soul. What could be more holy than this?
-   Make it a habit: When you feel fear, acknowledge it. Then do the thing you are afraid of without hesitation. Do not wait, do not bargain, convince, make excuses, or delay. Just do it, and do it now. Soon you'll be done, and you'll have acheived the greatest victory: to assert control over the instincts which sabotage you. "But I'm afraid, how do I do it?" By doing it. You do have a choice. You'll be dead soon. Death waits. You have no choice over that. But you do have a choice over your actions. Do not let your mind convince you otherwise.
-   Do not hesitate. It must happen now. Does death always come with a warning? You must always be ready.
-   If you fail to gather the courage to do it, respond accordingly:
    1. Forgive yourself. It will happen
    2. Remind yourself: You failed for one reason and one reason alone. You didn't have the courage to do it. _The moment wasn't right. She left before I could talk to her. I was waiting until the song ended. I didn't want to get hurt. It didn't go the way I planned._ Nope! You didn't make it happen. The opportunity is never right. But your mind wasn't either.
    3. Forget it, and be better next time. Once you take total responsibility for the failure, move on.
-   Every day you will have at least one opportunity to do something scary. You don't see it? Your mind has shackled and blinded you. Realistically, there's at least 5 opportunities in a day to do something you fear. To find them just ask: If I were going to die in one week, what would I do differently right now? Then do it. You can't because you're not actually dying? Yes you are. Just more slowly. Go do it.

### Talking with Yourself

-   Talk to yourself. Have conversations. Encourage yourself. Whisper the truth in your own ear.
-   Ask
    -   "How am I doing now?"
    -   "Are my needs getting met now?"
    -   "Am I taking care of myself?"
    -   "What am I doing?"
-   Tell
    -   "If this is death..."
    -   "Come on bro, you'll be dead soon, just do it"
    -   "You're better than this, man"
    -   "Find it within yourself"
    -   "The universe is change, life is perception"
    -   "Man suffers only because he takes seriously what the gods made for fun"
-   Use the power of words to your advantage. Remember quotes you hear that help point your mind towards truth.
-   Write poetry, commit it to memory, and use it as a mantra. Remember all you have gained from this practice, and remember the words you will say as life exits your body.

### Reflecting

-   At the beginning of the day:
    -   What are my goals for today?
    -   If I did just one thing well today, what should it be?
    -   What struggles will I face today and how do I want to handle them?
-   At the end of the day:
    -   What did I do well today?
    -   What could I have done better?
    -   If every day were like today, would I be okay with that? If not, what needs to change?
    -   Did I uphold my values today?
    -   How am I better right now than I was 24 hours ago?
    -   What am I grateful for?
-   When you neglect to this this, treat the day as wasted.

### Conclusion

Note: Update this list frequently, and refer to it often. You've always known that you may die sooner than you expect. Be ready. When you die, let your last thought be:

_"I am not afraid, for I have mastered my mind, and with it, have seen the greatest truth, beauty, and power that the universe has to offer. I now die, but I have lived, and I have seen the face of God. So I shall walk into the sunset knowing that all is well, and I have fulfilled what I was meant to do."_

Are these words true for you? Make them so.
